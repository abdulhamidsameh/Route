The first volleyball team of Zamalek Club “women” defeated its counterpart, the Rwandan army, 3-0, in the match that was held between them at the “Nabeul” hall in Tunisia, as part of the first round of Group D of the African Clubs Championship, which Tunisia will host from 12 to 23 May.

The first handball team defeated the Kinshasa youth team 56/29 in the third match of the African Handball Cup Winners' Cup. The White Knight led the Kinshasa team in the first half, with a score of 15/27 The team will play tomorrow, Sunday, May 14, with the Wydad Samara team, as part of the team’s last meeting in the group stage of the African Cup Winners’ Cup, and the match will be held at exactly seven o’clock in the evening at the Al-Ahly club hall

Amr El-Sisi, the midfielder of the first football team at Zamalek Club, participated in the group training that was held today, Thursday, at the Abdel Latif Abourguila Stadium, at the club’s headquarters, in preparation for the upcoming Ismaili match in the Premier League competition. This came after the player completed the special rehabilitation program that he underwent during the last period, after recovering from the injury he was suffering from at the knee level.

Mahmoud Hamdi “Al-Wensh”, defender of the first football team in Zamalek Club, performed individual training at Cairo International Stadium, after the end of the proxy match in the 32nd round of the Egypt Cup. The coaching staff, led by Colombian Juan Carlos Osorio, asked Al-Winch to undergo individual training after the match to prepare him for the next period, especially since he did not participate in today's match.

The coaching staff of the Zamalek club's first football team, led by Colombian Juan Carlos Osorio, refused to give the players a break from group training tomorrow, Wednesday, after defeating Proxy in the Egypt Cup. Osorio decided that the team would go into training tomorrow, Wednesday, at exactly nine o’clock in the morning, at Abdel Latif Abu Rujaila Stadium, at the White Castle headquarters.

Colombian Juan Carlos Osorio, coach of the first football team at Zamalek Club, confirmed that the cup matches in all countries of the world serve as an opportunity for the teams that participate in the second division leagues to present strong matches. The coach explained in the press conference for the match that he studied the opponent well, and that he did not reduce his size, adding that he pushed a formation of young players, and gave the opportunity to others, such as Samson, Mahmoud Shabana and Youssef Osama Nabih. Osorio said: “The adjustments made by the coaching staff between the two halves contributed to improving the team’s level, and there were individual errors in the match that caused Zamalek to concede goals.”
